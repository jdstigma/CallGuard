# FCC Complaint — Draft (Caller ID Spoofing / Harassing Calls)

File at: **https://consumercomplaints.fcc.gov** → **Phone** → **Unwanted Calls**
→ issue type **Caller ID Spoofing**.

This is a blank template. Fill every placeholder in [BRACKETS] before submitting —
including the call totals, which you take from your own CallGuard summary.

## Description (paste into the complaint's free-text box)

> I am receiving a sustained campaign of harassing phone calls to my cell phone,
> [YOUR CELL NUMBER]. The calls come from a constantly changing set of numbers,
> which strongly indicates caller ID spoofing.
>
> I have been keeping a precise, timestamped log of every call. Over the logged
> period ([FIRST DATE] to [MOST RECENT DATE]) I have recorded **[TOTAL CALLS] calls
> from [UNIQUE NUMBERS] different phone numbers**. Of these, **[FLAGGED COUNT] match
> a clear harassment pattern**: incoming calls from numbers not in my contacts on
> which the caller remains completely **silent** and/or hangs up within a few
> seconds. [If applicable: A disproportionate number arrive during overnight hours,
> around [BUSIEST HOUR]:00, when legitimate calls are not expected.]
>
> The use of [UNIQUE NUMBERS] distinct originating numbers to place [TOTAL CALLS]
> calls is not consistent with any legitimate caller and is consistent with
> deliberate caller ID spoofing intended to harass and to evade blocking.
>
> I have taken the following steps:
> - I filed / am filing a police report ([CASE NUMBER IF YOU HAVE IT]).
> - I opened a harassment case with my carrier, [CARRIER], ([CARRIER CASE NUMBER]).
> - I maintain a full CSV log and a summary report of every call, which I can
>   provide, and which supports a carrier/industry **traceback** of the
>   originating provider.
>
> I am requesting that the FCC investigate this spoofing campaign and that the
> originating provider be identified through the traceback process. I can supply
> my complete call log on request.
