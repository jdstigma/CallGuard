# AT&T (Wireless) — Harassment Call Playbook & Phone Script

Important reality for **mobile**: `*57` Call Trace is a **landline** feature and
is not reliable on AT&T wireless. On a cell phone, the path that actually
unmasks a spoofed caller is: **you file a police report → the police subpoena
AT&T → AT&T hands over the true originating records.** Your CallGuard CSV is the
evidence that starts that chain. AT&T's own tools below are for **blocking and
documentation**, not for revealing the caller to you directly.

---

## Step 1 — Turn on AT&T ActiveArmor (free, blocks + screens)

- Install **AT&T ActiveArmor** (formerly "AT&T Call Protect") from the Play Store.
- Sign in with your AT&T account.
- Turn on **Spam Risk blocking** and **Unknown Caller** handling.
- This won't identify a spoofed number, but it cuts the volume and logs spam
  categories. CallGuard keeps your evidence log in parallel.

## Step 2 — Call AT&T and open a documented harassment case

**Dial 611** from your AT&T phone, or **1-800-331-0500**. Ask to be transferred
to **fraud / harassment support**.

### Word-for-word script

> "I'm an AT&T wireless customer and I'm being harassed by repeated calls. Each
> call comes from a different number and the caller stays silent on the line — I
> believe the numbers are spoofed. I want to:
> 1. Open a documented harassment case on my account,
> 2. Get a reference/ticket number for it, and
> 3. Know exactly what records AT&T will release to law enforcement once I file
>    a police report, so the officer knows what to subpoena."

Then ask directly:

> "If I bring a police report and case number, what's the process for AT&T to
> provide the originating call records for these calls?"

**Write down:** the AT&T case/ticket number, the rep's name, and the date/time.
You'll give the case number to the police.

## Step 3 — File the police report (this is the unmasking step)

- Bring your **CallGuard CSV export** (dates, times, ring durations, the silent
  pattern) and the **AT&T case number** from Step 2.
- Tell the officer: "The numbers are spoofed; AT&T will release the true
  originating records under subpoena — here's my carrier case number."
- Get a **police report number**.

## Step 4 — Federal filings (spoofing is regulated)

- **FCC:** https://consumercomplaints.fcc.gov  (choose "Unwanted Calls" →
  "Caller ID Spoofing")
- **FTC:** https://reportfraud.ftc.gov

---

## What to have ready before you call AT&T
- Your AT&T account PIN / passcode (they'll verify you).
- The latest **CallGuard CSV** (export it right before calling).
- A pen — case number, rep name, date/time.

## What NOT to do
- Don't speak to or engage the silent caller — just log and hang up.
- Don't expect AT&T to tell YOU who it is on the phone; that comes through the
  police/subpoena route for privacy/legal reasons.
