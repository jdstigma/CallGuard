# Harassment Call — Action Guide (Carrier + Police)

This is the part that actually **unmasks** the caller. An app on your phone can
only record what the network shows it (which for a spoofed call is the *fake*
number). Only your carrier and law enforcement can trace the real origin. Do
these steps in parallel with using the CallGuard app to build your evidence log.

---

## 1. Immediately after a harassing call: dial **\*57** (Call Trace)

Most US carriers support **Call Trace**. Right after the harassing call ends,
**before** any other call comes in:

1. Pick up the phone and dial **`*57`** then call.
2. You'll hear a confirmation tone/message.
3. This tells the **carrier** to log the true originating line for that specific
   call in a way police can subpoena. **You won't see the result** — it goes to
   the carrier/law enforcement, by design.

> Note: \*57 usually has a small per-use fee and must be done immediately after
> each call. It's the single most useful thing you can do in the moment.

---

## 2. Call your carrier's harassment / fraud department

Say (adapt as needed):

> "I'm receiving repeated harassing calls from different numbers — the caller
> stays silent on the line. I believe the numbers are spoofed. I want to open a
> harassment case, have these calls traced, and get guidance on next steps. I'm
> keeping a timestamped log of every call."

Carrier contacts:
- **Verizon:** *611 or 1-800-922-0204 — ask for the harassment/fraud dept.
- **AT&T:** 1-800-331-0500 — AT&T Call Protect + Global Fraud Management.
- **T-Mobile:** 611 or 1-800-937-8997 — Scam Shield team.
- Prepaid/MVNO: use the support number on your carrier's site.

Ask specifically about: a **Call Trace order**, and whether they can enable
**anonymous/unknown-caller blocking** on your line at the network level.

---

## 3. File a police report (do this early)

Harassment via phone is a crime in every US state. Police can **subpoena the
carrier** for the traced-call records — that's how spoofed numbers get unmasked.

Bring:
- Your **CallGuard CSV export** (dates, times, ring durations, the silent
  pattern).
- Any context on who you suspect and why.
- The **\*57** trace dates (tell them you ran traces; the carrier has the data).

Ask for a **case/report number** — you'll give it to your carrier so they can
release records to that investigation.

---

## 4. File with the FCC and FTC (spoofing is federally regulated)

- **FCC (spoofing/robocalls):** https://consumercomplaints.fcc.gov
- **FTC:** https://reportfraud.ftc.gov

These build a federal paper trail and can trigger enforcement if it's part of a
larger operation.

---

## 5. Practical protection while the case proceeds

- Turn on **"Silence Unknown Callers"** (Android: Phone app → Settings → Caller
  ID & spam / Blocked numbers). The CallGuard app still logs them for evidence.
- Do **not** engage or speak during silent calls — just log and hang up.
- Keep your evidence log continuous; gaps weaken a case.

---

## Evidence log — what makes it hold up

For each call, the CallGuard app records: **number shown, exact timestamp, ring
duration, answered/missed/rejected, and your note**. Consistency (same silent
pattern, clustered times, unknown numbers) is what convinces a carrier and a
judge to act. Export the CSV before every carrier call and police visit.
